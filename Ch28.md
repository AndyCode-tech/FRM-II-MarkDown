# Ch.28 STRUCTURED CREDIT RISK

## 28.1: STRUCTURED PRODUCTS

### Types of Structured Products

## 📌 Core Definitions

-   **Securitization:** Pooling credit-sensitive assets to create new securities (structured products) with risk/return profiles that differ from the original underlying assets.
    
-   **Structured Products / Portfolio Credit Products:** The resulting securities whose cash flows are derived from the underlying pool of loans or credit claims.
    

## 🏢 1. Covered Bonds (On-Balance Sheet)

-   **Structure:** Dual-recourse, **on-balance sheet** securitizations.
    
-   **Collateral:** A "covered pool" of mortgages separated on the originator’s balance sheet.
    
-   **Bankruptcy Status:** **Not a true securitization.** Assets are _not_ part of a bankruptcy-remote structure.
    
-   **Investor Protection:** * Investors have higher priority than general creditors if the bank defaults.
    
    -   P&I is guaranteed by the originator—payments do _not_ depend on the pool's performance. Investors have recourse against the originator.
        

## 🏡 2. Mortgage Pass-Through Securities (True MBS)

-   **Structure:** True **off-balance sheet** securitization.
    
-   **Cash Flows:** Based entirely on the performance of the pool (minus servicing fees).
    
-   **Risks:**
    
    -   **Default Risk:** Very low. Most are Agency MBS with implicit or explicit government guarantees.
        
    -   **Primary Risk:** **Prepayment risk** (homeowners refinancing when interest rates fall or selling homes).
        

## 🌊 3. Collateralized Mortgage Obligations (CMOs)

-   **Structure:** MBS that **tranches (divides)** cash flows into different securities with distinct maturities, fixed/floating rates, or specific conditions.
    
-   **The Sequential Pay (Waterfall) Structure:**
    
    -   _Tranche 1_ receives **all** principal payments + its portion of interest until fully paid off.
        
    -   _Remaining Tranches_ receive **interest only** until the preceding tranche is completely retired.
        
    -   **Exam Note:** Tranche 1 has the shortest lifespan and highest prepayment risk, while later tranches face extension risk.
        

## 📉 4. Structured Credit Products (Credit Tranching)

-   **Structure:** Asset pools backed by risky debt instruments, specifically tranched by **credit risk** (default risk) rather than prepayment time.
    
-   **The Credit Waterfall:**
    
    -   **Junior / Equity Tranche:** Bears the **first losses** from defaulted assets. Highest risk, highest potential return, most likely to be written down.
        
    -   **Mezzanine Tranches:** Absorb subsequent losses once the equity tranche is completely wiped out.
        
    -   **Senior Tranche:** Highest credit rating, lowest probability of write-downs, paid first.
        

## 🧳 5. Asset-Backed Securities (ABS)

-   **Definition:** The most general class of securitizations where cash-flow generating assets are pooled and tranched. _MBS, CMOs, CBOs, CDOs, and CLOs are all sub-types of ABS._
    
-   **CDO-Squared ($CDO^2$):** Highly complex securities created by pooling _other_ existing CDOs together.
    

### ⚙️ Dimensions of ABS Variance

1.  **Underlying Collateral:** Can be loans, bonds, credit card receivables, auto loans, or non-debt cash flows (e.g., toll road collections).
    
2.  **Structure:** The size and number of tranches are specific to each unique transaction.
    
3.  **Management Style:**
    
    -   **Passive Pool:** Assets (like mortgages or auto loans) naturally pay themselves down over time.
        
    -   **Actively Managed Pool:** A manager selectively buys/sells assets to exploit overvalued or undervalued loans to enhance performance.
        
    -   **Revolving Pool:** During a specific "revolving period," loan proceeds are reinvested into new assets. Once the period ends, the asset balances become fixed (e.g., credit card balances) and amortize down.
