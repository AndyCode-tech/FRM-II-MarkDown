# Credit Scoring and Retail Credit Risk Management

## Creditworthiness
### Mortgage Credit Assessment 
- FICO score
	- numerical socre --> measure of default risk base on credit history
- Loan-to-value (LTV) ratio
		-  amount of mortgage divided by the associated property's total appraised value
- Debt-to-income (DTI) ratio: 
	- ratio of monthly debt payments (mortgage, auto) to the monthly gross income of the borrower
- Payment type:
	- adjustable rate / fixed 
- Document types
	- Full doc: a loan which requires evidence of assets and income.
	- Stated income: employment is veri ied but borrower income is not.
	- No ratio: similar to stated income, employment is documented but income is not. The debt-to-income ratio is not calculated.
	- No income/no asset: income and assets are provided on the loan application but are not lender veri ied (other than the source of income).
	- No doc: no documentation of income or assets is provided.

### Cutoff Scores
- Thresholds where leanders determine they will lend money or not
- After setting cutoff score --> use historical experience to establish the estimated profitability for specific product line and associated loss rate 
- Use longer timeframe --> capture a full economic cycle --> maximize the balance between risk and profitability

- Banks need to group their portfolios into subgroups that share similar loss attributes --> differentiate the groups by risk levels.
- For each bands --> calculate the PD and LGD

## Tradeoff Between Creditworthiness and Profitability
- Credit Card for high FICO score ppl --> pay bill regularly --> no interest from customers 
- Use product and customers profit scoring to evaluate profitability 
- Other measures 
	- Revenue score --> existing customers profiability
	- Application score --> extend credit to a new applicant
	- Response score --> prob a customer will respond to an offer
	- Insurance score --> prob of a claims by the insured
	- Behavious score --> existing customer credit usage and historical delinquencies 
	- Tax authority scores --> prob. of audits may be needed for revenue collection
	- Attrition score --> prob. to the reduction or elimination of oustanding debt by existing customers

## Risk Based Pricing
- Adverse selection <-- using a singal price for all customers 
- Using risk based pricing (RBP)
	- new method --> frequently used in credit card, home mortgage and auto loan lines
- Factors affecting RBP
	- The probability of take-up (i.e., acceptance by the customer of the offered product). 
	- The probability of default (PD). 
	- The loss given default (LGD). 
	- The exposure at default (EAD).
	- The cost of equity capital to the lender. Capital allocated to the transaction.
- After having the pricing strategy
	- map it to metrics such as profit/loss, revenue, market share, risk-afjusted return at the various score bands
