# CH24: Country Risk: Determinants, Measures and Implications

## Country Risk
- Increasingly exposed to country risk 
	- Investing in nondomestic companies is easier
	- Large range for investment (mutual funds, ETFs, shares of foreign stock that are traded on domestic exchagnes [American depositary receipts (ADRs)], [Global Depositary Receipts (GDRs)]
- Companies are increasingly global
- Key sources of country risk
	- Where the country is in the economic growth life cycle
	- Political risks
	- legal systems of countries (structure and efficiency)
	- Disproportionate reliance on one product / commodity or service

### Political Risk
1. Continuous vs. discontinuous risk
	- Some investors perfer stability -->countries with autocratic leadership (one individual exerts significant control over decision-making with minimal input from group members) --> policies are perdictable, changes will be more easier. 
	- But costs of corruption and lack of legal systems
	- Risk in decomcraies are continuous but generally low 
	- Risk in dictatorshups are discountinuous 
		- Change is not oftern but severe and difficult to protect against

2. Corruption 
	- likely to have implicit tax 
	- Legal sanctions 

3. Physical violence
	- Economic costs (insurance and security costs) 
	- Physical costs (physical harm to employees or investors)

4. Nationalization and expropriation risk (國有化/徵用)
	- Profits will be expropriated via arbitrary taxation by governments 
	- Nationalized --> receive much less than the true value of the company

### Legal Risk
- Protection of property rights (Structure of the legal system)
- Speed for disputed get settled (Efficiency of the legal System)
- Protection bu the legal system is the risk

### Economic Structure 
- Disproportionate reliance on a single commodity or service
- Senstive to changes in commodity prices

# Sovereign Default Risk
- Holders of government-issued debt fail to receive the full amount of promised interest and principal payment during the specified time period.
- Sovereign Default Risk can be used as a proxy for country risk
- Foreign currency defaults and local currency defaults

## Foreign Currency Defaults 
- Borrowed money from other countries or banks
- The government is not having enough foreign currency and unable to print money in a foreign currency to repay the debt
- Countries were more likely to default on funds borrowed from banks than on sovereign bond issues.
- Latin America accounts for a large proportion of sovereign defaults in the last 50 years (measured in dollar value terms)

## Local Currency Defaults
- If local defaults --> oftern foreign default
Factors explaining local currency defaults
- Gold standard 
	- need to have gold to back up the currency
- Shared Currency
	- unable to print money 
	- example: EU
- The tradeoff 
	- printing money --> devalue and debase the currency
	- Higher inflation
	- What extent foreign currency debt is funding local currency assets
		- the greater the extent --> more negative the consequences
		- Reducing asset values while leavinf liabilities unchanged 

## Consequences of Sovereign Default
- Defaults were often followed by military actions
- Loss of reputation --> difficult and more expensive to borrow in the future
- Turmoil in stock and bond markets
- Decrease in real output and deal with political instability
- Default Result
	- Restructuring of debt
	- GDP decline - 0.5% to -2.0% in the first year
	- Lower sovereign ratings and higher borrowing costs
	- Trade retaliation: Drop in 8% bilateral trade, last up to 15 years
	- More fragile banking systems: 14% prob banking crisis, normal is 3%
	- Increased likelihood of political change
